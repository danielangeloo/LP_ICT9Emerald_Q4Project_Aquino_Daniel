CHARACTERS IN NOLI ME TANGERE
Crisostomo Ibarra
  • His full name is Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra y Magsalin.
  • a well-off young man of mixed Spanish and Filipino descent. Ibarra is well-liked, but the friars in San Diego are wary of him.
  • He Studied abroad in Europe for his education for seven years.
Maria Clara
  • Maria Clara, her full name is Maria Clara de los Santos.
  • Has a high social status in San Diego, she is also the daughter of Kapitan Tiago.
  • Maria Clara and Crisostomo Ibarra, who were childhood friends, are now engaged and are getting married.
Elias
  • Elias was a wanted man who resided in San Diego. After seeing tragedy after tragedy, he started trying to make society better.
  • He started helping Crisostomo Ibarra after he saved his life.
Kapitan Tiago
  • His full name is Don Santiago de los Santos, he is the father of Maria Clara
  • a well-liked and respected socialite from the rich class. One of the wealthiest landowners in Manila and San Diego.
Padre Damaso
  • A Spanish priest living in the Philippines.
  • Father Damaso is an arrogant priest who hardly speaks or understands Tagalog, despite spending more than twenty years living among Filipinos and listening to their confessions.
Padre Salvi
  • A serious and dedicated Spanish friar succeeds Father Damaso as the town's priest in San Diego.
  • Being a greedy and self-centered person, he enjoys imposing heavy taxes on the people.
Don Rafael Ibarra
  • He is the father of Crisostomo Ibarra, he had died even before the start of Noli Me Tangere.
  • He was imprisoned for a crime he didn't even commit, he had passed away in jail even before they could clear his name.
Pilosopo Tasyo o Don Anastacio
  • Pilosopo Tasio, short for Anastacio, was an old scholar living in San Diego.
  • He was regarded as a crazy for his unusual beliefs, yet he ended up being a local advisor for various people.
Sisa
  • Sisa is the mother of Basilio and Crispin and a resident of San Diego.
  • Sisa went crazy after both of her boys disappeared and wandered the town looking for them.
Crispin
  • Along with his brother Basilio, Crispin served as a sacristan for the church while he was a young kid living in San Diego.
  • Crispin was disciplined by the head sacristan and Padre Salvi because Basilio and Crispin's father had committed several wrong doings such as excessive drinking and because of that, Crispin was suspected of stealing money from the church.
Basilio
  • The older brother of Crispin, Basilio, was a young boy who lived in San Diego.
  • Along with his brother Crispin, Basilio served as a sacristan for the church.
Donya Victorina de Espadaña
  • Her full name is La Doctora Victorina de los Reyes de Espadaña but more commonly known as Donya Victorina.
  • Donya Victorina was a well-known socialite and Capitan Tiago's friend.
Don Tiburcio de Espadaña
  • His full name is Doctor Tiburcio de Espadaña but is more commonly known as Don Tiburcio de Espadaña and is the husband of Donya Victorina
  • a Spanish man who has a stutter when he speaks and appears to be much older than his thirty-five years. Don Tiburcio was hired as a customs officer before arriving in the Philippines, but he was soon fired.
Donya Consolacion
  • An older Filipina woman married to El Alférez, she is said to have no love for her country.
  • Dona Consolación is portrayed by Jose Rizal as being extremely rude and unattractive.
Alperes
  • A Spaniard in charge of the Civil Guard in San Diego. The ensign has a bitter relationship with Padre Salvi, since he thinks Padre Salví takes his position too seriously.
  • He fights with his difficult wife, Dona Consolación, a Filipina, every day of their marriage.
Tiya Isabel
  • Kapitan Tiago’s cousin.
  • The woman who took care of Mara Clara after her mother died giving delivery.
